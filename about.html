About Me

My name is Santiago Hernandez, I am 3rd year student at Syracuse University. I love learning about the world, and a big part of that has been travel. Though I've traveled my fair share, I'm certainly not rich; I'm an immigrant and first-generation college student, and mostly rely on financial aid in order to get through college. There are three things that have allowed me to travel without breaking the bank...

Scholarships

A few months ago, I was able to study abroad in Berlin. Studying abroad can be expensive, but I was able to afford through scholarships. I applied to and received study abroad grants at my school, in addition to the Gilman Scholarship, a government-run program that gives college students $5000 to spend as they please.

Budget Travel

When I travelled solo for the first time ever (to Vietnam), I knew that instead of spending my money on nice clothes, luxury hotels, and fancy restaurants, I wanted to spend it on experiences. I found the cheapest all-inclusive volunteer program in Hanoi, packed a few few clothes, and off I went.

School

School is the last thing most people need to be able to travel, but in my case, it helped a lot. If it wasn't for school, I wouldn't have had access to scholarships.
