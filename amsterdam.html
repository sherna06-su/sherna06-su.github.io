Amsterdam, Netherlands

Amsterdam is the Netherlands’ capital, known for its artistic heritage, elaborate canal system and narrow houses with gabled facades, legacies of the city’s 17th-century Golden Age. Its Museum District houses the Van Gogh Museum, works by Rembrandt and Vermeer at the Rijksmuseum, and modern art at the Stedelijk. Cycling is key to the city’s character, and there are numerous bike paths.

Top things to see:

Shops

Amsterdam is famous for the variety of unique shops that one can find. From a store entirely dedicated to different kinds of rubber ducks (pictured) to a store entirely dedicated to differnt kinds of condoms and related items, to stores dedicated to different kinds of magic mushrooms, you are bound to find what you are looking for.

Red Light District

From brothels to sex shops to museums, the Amsterdam Red Light District has it all. But contrary to what a lot of people might think, the Amsterdam Red Light District has a very friendly atmosphere and isn't as dangerous as it used to be. From "peep shows" to regulated prostitution to sex theaters where bananas are extensively used (pictured), you will not find a place like this one anywhere else!

Westerpark

Westerpark blends green spaces, a lively creative and nightlife scene, and iconic architecture. Along with its regular exhibitions, markets and festivals, the converted Westergasfabriek gasworks contains indie shops, eclectic eateries, an art-house cinema, a brewery and a dance club.
