Narita, Japan

Narita is a city on the eastern outskirts of Tokyo, Japan. It's home to Tokyoâ€™s Narita International Airport. In Narita's center is the centuries-old Naritasan Shinshoji Temple, a Buddhist complex with gilded pagodas and tranquil gardens. I had a 12-hour layover at Narita International Airport, and through an airport program where locals give FREE tours to travelers, I was led through the town by a sweet, 70-year old Japanese man.

Top things to see:

Omotesando

Running between Narita train station and the temple is Omotesando, a narrow street lined with colorful shops and restaurants in the style of Japan's Edo period. In a very Japanese fashion, the street is fitted with speakers that play classical music, and you will find all kinds of foods you are likely to never see again!

Naritasan Shinshoki Temple

In Narita's center is the centuries-old Naritasan Shinshoji Temple, a Buddhist complex with gilded pagodas and tranquil gardens. Through my tour guide, I was able to enter various temples and observe ongoing ceremonies.

Gardens

One of the nicest parts of the Naritasan Temple is the surrounding gardens where temple visitors are able to get lost in the winding trails and transquil streams.
