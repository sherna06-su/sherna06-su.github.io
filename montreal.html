Montreal, Canada

Montréal is the largest city in Canada's Québec province. It’s set on an island in the Saint Lawrence River and named after Mt. Royal, the triple-peaked hill at its heart. Its boroughs, many of which were once independent cities, include neighbourhoods ranging from cobblestoned, French colonial Vieux-Montréal – with the Gothic Revival Notre-Dame Basilica at its centre – to bohemian Plateau.

Top things to do:

Rent a moped

Montreal has a lot to offer...if you're running short on time, renting a moped (pictured) would allow you to see many of the sights faster, and in a more fun way! Sights could include visiting McGill University to riding up Mount Royal, a small mountain where you could see the whole city.

Notre-Dame Basilica of Montreal

Notre-Dame Basilica is a basilica in the historic district of Old Montreal. The interior of the church is amongst the most dramatic in the world and regarded as a masterpiece of Gothic Revival architecture.[1] The vaults are coloured deep blue and decorated with golden stars, and the rest of the sanctuary is decorated in blues, azures, reds, purples, silver, and gold.

Visit a street market

Montreal has an array of flea and street markets. It is not unsual to find that there are miles of street blocks closed in order to make way for a street market, and the many unique foods offered.
