Hanoi, Vietnam

Hanoi, the capital of Vietnam, is known for its centuries-old architecture and a rich culture with Southeast Asian, Chinese and French influences. I spent a month volunteering for an NGO just outside of the "old quarter", the heart of the city and the center of trade. From the craziest traffic in the world, to a thriving street-food culture, to a close proximity to natural wonders, Hanoi is the the most exciting place I've been to.

Top things to do:

Motorcycle Tour

The best way to get to know the real Hanoi, is through a guided motorcycle tour. Through sites like Trip Advisor, its possible to pick a high-quality tour at a bargain price. The tour I went on was vintage Russian motorcycles, and in the span of 8 hours, took me to see everything from the French Quarter, to one of the first restaurants to serve noodles, to the resting place of Ho Chi Minh.

Ha Long Bay

Hạ Long Bay, 4 hours away from Hanoi, is known for its emerald waters and thousands of towering limestone islands topped by rainforests. Junk boat tours and sea kayak expeditions take visitors past islands named for their shapes, including Stone Dog and Teapot islets. I spent 3 days aboard one of the "junk boats" and did everything from watching the beatiful sunset, to visiting a pearl farm, to kayaking and exploring hidden caves.

Sa Pa

Sa Pa is a town 6 hours away from Hanoi in the Hoàng Liên Son Mountains of northwestern Vietnam. A popular trekking base, it overlooks the terraced rice fields of the Muong Hoa Valley, and is near the 3,143m-tall Phang Xi Pang peak, which is climbable via a steep, multiday guided walk. I went on a 3-day hike led by a native woman who had never left the valley, and I got to stay in homestays each night where the only bevarage available was usually rice wine!

Old Quarter

Packed with charming colonial architecture, Buddhist temples and pagodas, the Old Quarter, located near Hoan Kiem Lake in Hoan Kiem District, is Hanoi’s major commercial district. Its heart and soul is exposed in the ancient commercial streets which are named after their original businesses dating back about 1,000 years. From outdoor bars to markets where you could bargain for the nicest fake Ray Bans you've ever seen, the old quarter is the heart of the city.
